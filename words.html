My Big Book of Big Words!

Accrue:
    1. To increase over a period of time, or receive an amount of something
   gradually over time


Anagapesis:
    1. (Noun) Falling out of love
    ("I've fallen into an anagapesis with this toy.")


Belap:
    1. Lap, wrap around or envelope something/someone.


Categorematic:
    1. Word or term capable of bein g understood on its own,
    without connecting it to other ideas. (Adjective)


Conflagrant:
    1. Burning; on fire. (Adjective)


Constituent:
    1. A component of something.
    ("The Linux kernel is only a constituent of the operating system.")


Discandy:
    1. To melt, dissolve, or thaw.


Fard:
    1. Facial cosmetics (like makeup) (Noun)
    2. To apply cosmetics (makeup; paint) to the face. (Verb)


Freewheeling / Freewheelingness:
    1. Not limited by rules or conventions. (Adjective)


Hemoid:
    1. Resembling blood


Illaqueate:
    1. To entrap, ensnare, entangle, or catch.


Jaunt:
    1. Excursion or journey for pleasure.


Laodicean:
    1. To be lukewarm/half-hearted or indifferent, especially toward
    religion or politics


Pinguid:
    1. Fatty, oily, or greasy (Adjective)


Repine:
    1. To feel or express dejection or discontent/sadness.


Sesquipedalian:
    1. Having many syllables
    2. Characterized as using long words
    ("The sesquipedalian announcer droned on.") (Adjective)


Snotter:
    1. To cry or whine. ("'I wish to go home!' I snottered")
    2. To smack or hit. (colloquial)


Syncategorematic:
    1. Needing to be connected with other terms, words or ideas to be understood.     (Antonym of "Categorematic") (Adjective)


Vaunt:
    1. To boast or praise something, especially in excess.
    ("Their much vaunted cyber security team let the users' data get leaked.")
